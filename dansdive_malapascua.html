Discover Malapascua Island

Malapascua Island in the Philippines


is renowned for its exceptional diving, especially for thresher shark encounters. It offers rich marine biodiversity, including vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life. The island's dive sites cater to all levels of divers and feature opportunities for macro photography and wreck diving. With year-round diving conditions, Malapascua is a top destination for unforgettable underwater experiences.

Malapascua Island is a diver’s paradise, attracting enthusiasts from around the globe. The island’s most famous attraction is the thresher shark, which can be seen almost daily at Monad Shoal. These majestic creatures, with their long, whip-like tails, are a sight to behold and provide a thrilling experience for divers.

Beyond thresher sharks, Malapascua boasts a rich marine ecosystem. The coral reefs are teeming with life, from colorful nudibranchs and pygmy seahorses to schools of barracuda and jacks. The island’s dive sites, such as Gato Island and Kemod Shoal, offer a variety of underwater landscapes, including caves, tunnels, and vibrant coral gardens.

Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced diver, Malapascua has something to offer. The island’s dive centers provide training and certification courses, ensuring that even novice divers can enjoy the underwater wonders. For advanced divers, there are opportunities for deep dives, night dives, and even technical diving.

Malapascua is also a haven for macro photographers. The island’s dive sites are home to a plethora of tiny, fascinating creatures perfect for close-up shots. Additionally, the island offers wreck diving experiences, such as the Dona Marilyn wreck, a sunken ferry that has become an artificial reef, attracting a variety of marine life.

One of the many advantages of diving in Malapascua is the year-round favorable conditions. The island enjoys a tropical climate, with warm waters and good visibility throughout the year. This makes it an ideal destination for divers looking to explore the underwater world at any time.

While diving is the main attraction, Malapascua Island has much more to offer. The island’s pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and laid-back atmosphere make it a perfect spot for relaxation. Visitors can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, island hopping, and exploring the local culture and cuisine.

Malapascua Island is a must-visit destination for diving enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. Its unique combination of thrilling shark encounters, rich marine biodiversity, and diverse dive sites ensures an unforgettable experience. Whether you’re an avid diver or simply looking for a tropical getaway, Malapascua Island promises adventure and tranquility in equal measure.


A Diver’s Paradise

Scuba Diving in Malapascua Island


Scuba Diving in Malapascua is a diver’s paradise renowned for its stunning underwater landscapes and unique marine life. This small island offers an unforgettable diving experience, attracting enthusiasts from around the world.

Thresher Sharks: One of the main attractions of Malapascua is the opportunity to dive with thresher sharks. These majestic creatures are known for their long tails and are typically seen at Monad Shoal, a famous dive site where they come for cleaning at dawn. The thrill of encountering these elusive sharks in their natural habitat is a once-in-a-lifetime experience for many divers.

Beyond thresher sharks, Malapascua boasts a rich variety of marine life. Divers can encounter colorful coral reefs, sea turtles, nudibranchs, pygmy seahorses, and a plethora of tropical fish. The vibrant underwater ecosystem makes every dive a new adventure, with something exciting to discover at every turn.

The island is also home to the wreck of the Dona Marilyn, a ferry that sank in 1988. This site offers an exciting dive for those interested in exploring sunken vessels. The wreck has become an artificial reef, attracting a diverse array of marine life and providing a fascinating glimpse into the past.

A short boat ride from Malapascua, Gato Island is another must-visit dive site. Known for its underwater tunnels and caverns, it is home to white-tip reef sharks, sea snakes, and a variety of cephalopods. The island’s unique topography and abundant marine life make it a favorite among divers seeking adventure and exploration.

Diving in Malapascua is possible year-round, but the best time to visit is from November to May when the weather is most favorable. During these months, the sea conditions are calm, and visibility is at its best, enhancing the overall diving experience. Early morning dives are recommended for the best chance to see thresher sharks, as they are most active at dawn.

Malapascua caters to divers of all skill levels. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced diver, the island offers a range of dive sites to suit your abilities. Dive centers on the island provide training and certification courses, ensuring that even novice divers can safely enjoy the underwater wonders. For advanced divers, there are opportunities for deep dives, night dives, and technical diving.

While diving is the main attraction, Malapascua Island has much more to offer. The island’s pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and laid-back atmosphere make it a perfect spot for relaxation. Visitors can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, island hopping, and exploring the local culture and cuisine. The friendly local community and the island’s natural beauty create a welcoming and memorable experience for all visitors.

Malapascua Island is a must-visit destination for diving enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. Its unique combination of thrilling shark encounters, rich marine biodiversity, and diverse dive sites ensures an unforgettable experience. Whether you’re an avid diver or simply looking for a tropical getaway, Malapascua Island promises adventure and tranquility in equal measure.
